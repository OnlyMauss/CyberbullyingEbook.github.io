Cyberbullying — What it is & How to stop it

A short e‑book / guide to understand cyberbullying, protect your information and report abuse.

What is it? Effects Report Protect info How to stop it

What is cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is using digital devices (social media, messaging, email, forums, games) to harass, threaten, embarrass or exclude another person. It includes sending hurtful messages, spreading rumours, sharing private images without consent, creating fake profiles, or encouraging others to target someone.

Common forms

  • Harassing messages and insults
  • Sharing private photos or sensitive information
  • Impersonation and fake accounts
  • Exclusion from groups or online shaming

What are the effects of cyberbullying?

Victims can feel stressed, anxious, depressed, lose self‑esteem, avoid school or social situations, and in extreme cases think about self‑harm. Cyberbullying can have long‑lasting emotional and social consequences because content can spread quickly and persist online.

If you or someone you know shows signs of severe distress, contact local emergency services or a trusted adult immediately.

Why is it important to report the problem?

Reporting helps stop the abuse, protects others, and creates an official record. Platforms can remove content and suspend accounts. Reporting also connects victims to support and can be useful if further legal or school action is needed.

How to report:
  1. Take screenshots and save messages.
  2. Use the platform's report feature.
  3. Tell a trusted adult, school authority or employer.
  4. Contact local law enforcement for threats or illegal content.

How do I prevent my personal info from being used?

Privacy settings
Lock your accounts to friends only, review what is visible and remove old posts containing sensitive info.
Think before sharing
Don't post private photos, addresses, phone numbers or passwords.
Use strong passwords
Use unique passwords and enable two‑factor authentication for important accounts.
Be careful with strangers
Check unknown friend requests and never accept links or files from untrusted users.

How can I stop cyberbullying?

Practical steps you can do right now:

  • Block the bully and mute notifications.
  • Save evidence (screenshots, URLs, dates).
  • Report the profile and offensive posts to the platform.
  • Tell a parent, teacher, or trusted friend.
  • Seek professional help if the impact is strong.
